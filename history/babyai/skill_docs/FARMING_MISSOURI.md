## Expertise

### Missouri Growing Conditions
- USDA Zones 5b-7a across the state
- Average last frost: April 10-25 (varies by region)
- Average first frost: October 5-20
- Growing season: 170-200 days
- Annual rainfall: 35-50 inches, heaviest in spring
- Dominant soil types: loess (river bluffs), glacial till (north), residual clay (Ozarks)

### Key Crops (Missouri)
- **Corn**: Plant mid-April to mid-May. 100-120 day varieties. Watch for corn rootworm and European corn borer. Soil temp needs to be 50°F+ at 2-inch depth.
- **Soybeans**: Plant May-June after corn. 100-140 day maturity. Double-crop after wheat in southern MO.
- **Wheat**: Winter wheat planted September-October. Harvest June-July. Good rotation crop.
- **Hay/Fescue**: Dominant forage grass. First cutting May, second July, third September if rain allows.

### Traditional Wisdom (Captured from Local Farmers)
- "Plant corn when oak leaves are the size of a squirrel's ear"
- Watch for persimmon seed shape in fall — spoon = heavy snow, fork = mild winter
- Soil ready to work when you can squeeze a handful and it crumbles, not clumps
- First heavy fog in August means first frost in exactly 90 days (local folk rule)
- Cattle lying down doesn't always mean rain — watch their grazing patterns instead

### Soil Health Indicators
- Earthworm count: 10+ per shovel = healthy soil biology
- Dark color and earthy smell = good organic matter
- Water infiltration: pour water, should absorb within 30 seconds
- Compaction: penetrometer or probe should push easily to 12 inches
- Cover crops (cereal rye, crimson clover) between cash crops build organic matter

### Pest and Disease Notes
- Japanese beetles peak late June through July
- Armyworms: watch for moth flights in late summer, scout field edges
- Sudden death syndrome in soybeans: cool, wet conditions after planting
- Gray leaf spot in corn: manage with rotation and resistant hybrids
