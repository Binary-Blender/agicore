Screamo – Intelligent Opportunity Scanner & Response Engine

Tagline: “Stop babysitting your inbox. Start weaponizing it.”

1. Product Overview

Screamo is an AI-powered communication orchestrator that:

Reads all your inbound channels:

Email (Gmail / O365 / IMAP)

LinkedIn DMs & comments

YouTube comments & DMs (where supported)

(Future) X/Twitter, Discord, etc.

Evaluates each message for:

Relevance

Opportunity value

Tone & intent

Prioritizes and routes:

Builds a single prioritized queue of “things Chris should see/respond to.”

Uses feedback + SPC to improve prioritization over time.

Responds intelligently:

Drafts replies in your style (using a personal KB & style model).

Supports different modes:

Standard reply (DM/email)

Agree & Amplify (supportive public comment)

Educate (polite, corrective “compliment sandwich”)

Battle Mode (firm but professional rebuttal).

Starts with human approval, then gradually reduces human involvement in well-controlled areas.

Goal: Make sure the right messages get your attention and the right responses go out — with less of your time in the loop over time.

2. Core Objectives

Unified Opportunity Inbox

Aggregate all messages into one normalized stream.

Surface the top 10–20 “high leverage” items at any given time.

Self-Improving Prioritization

Learn what “important” means for you.

Use feedback (approve, edit, discard, re-rank) to improve over time.

Apply Statistical Process Control (SPC) on key metrics (e.g., “accepted without edit” rate) to determine when automation can be safely increased.

On-Brand, On-Point Responses

Build a KB of your:

Writing style

Opinions

Big themes (AI orchestration, SPC, Amish, Binary Blender, etc.).

Draft responses that feel like “Chris on his best day with 8 hours of sleep and coffee.”

Outbound Engagement

Proactively scan your LinkedIn feed for:

Posts where you can Agree & Amplify.

Posts where you can Educate (gently “disagree,” but actually teach).

Handle replies and arguments, with escalation to Battle Mode when needed.

3. Key User Flows
3.1 Message Ingestion & Prioritization

Connect accounts:

User connects: Gmail/O365, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.

Screamo periodically pulls:

New emails, DMs, comments, mentions.

For each item:

Normalize into a unified schema (channel, author, timestamp, content, thread context, metadata).

Run Message Quality & Opportunity Scoring:

importance_score (0–100)

opportunity_type (job, partnership, sales lead, fan, hater, spam, etc.)

sentiment (supportive, neutral, negative, hostile)

Display in Screamo UI:

Inbox tab (prioritized queue)

Filters: Channel, opportunity type, sentiment, etc.

3.2 Feedback Loop & Learning

For each message, the user can:

Approve & send AI-drafted response.

Edit then send (capture the delta as training signal).

Reject AI response and:

Provide quick feedback tag: “Too aggressive”, “Too soft”, “Off-topic,” “Wrong priority,” “Don’t respond.”

Re-classify opportunity type if wrong.

Pin a message as “high importance” to teach what “high value” looks like.

All feedback feeds:

User preference model (tone, length, structure, risk tolerance).

Prioritization model (what to surface first).

SPC metrics (see below).

3.3 Response Modes
3.3.1 Standard Reply (DM / Email)

Draft personalized replies using:

Message content

Thread context

Your style KB

Known relationship/history (if available)

Offer:

Quick actions: “Shorten”, “More casual”, “More direct”, “More generous”, etc.

3.3.2 Agree & Amplify (Feed Comments)

Trigger condition:

Screamo finds a post in your feed with high alignment to your known beliefs and topics.

Behavior:

Generate a comment that:

Starts with authentic agreement (e.g., “100% this.”)

Adds a distinctive insight (not just “Great post!”).

Optionally includes a short example or “micro-framework” that sounds like you.

Control:

Early stage: All comments require your approval.

Later stage (post-SPC control): Low-risk comments can be auto-approved with periodic spot-checks.

3.3.3 Educate Mode (Polite Counterpoint)

Trigger condition:

Post is misaligned with your views, but not hostile.

Screamo sees an opportunity to add value by educating.

Behavior:

Use a compliment sandwich:

Positive opener (acknowledge something they got right or a legitimate concern).

Middle: Clarification, nuance, or alternative perspective.

Close: Encouragement or common-ground statement.

Example structure (internally):

Compliment: “You’re absolutely right that X is a huge concern…”

Education: “One nuance I’d add is that when you [explain your approach]…”

Wrap: “Curious how you’re seeing this play out in your own work.”

Guardrails:

No insults.

No quote-tweet-style dogpiling.

Emphasis on curiosity and clarity, not “winning.”

3.3.4 Battle Mode (Professional Dismantling)

Trigger condition:

A reply/mention/comment that:

Directly calls you out

Misrepresents your ideas

Or is aggressively dismissive.

Behavior:

“Battle Mode” is firm, factual, and surgical, not personal or abusive.

Steps:

Clarify the opponent’s main claims.

Deconstruct each claim with:

Evidence

Logic

Clear counterpoints

Re-assert your position succinctly.

Invite constructive debate or leave a mic-drop closer.

Rules:

No attacks on the person (only their arguments).

No profanity, slurs, or harassment.

Explicit “de-escalation preference” options in settings:

Soft Battle

Standard

Nuclear (still professional, but maximal argument density).

Control:

Battle mode always requires human approval for public posts.

SPC can only reduce friction in drafting (e.g., fewer internal checks), but not bypass your final review.

4. SPC Integration

We treat Screamo’s behavior as a process monitored with Statistical Process Control.

4.1 Key Metrics

Track over rolling windows:

Response Acceptance Rate

% of AI drafts sent without edits.

Light Edit Rate

% of drafts with minor edits (e.g., <10% token change).

Heavy Rewrite / Reject Rate

% of drafts the user completely rewrites or discards.

Misclassification Rate

% of messages the user re-tags (wrong opportunity type, wrong priority).

User Satisfaction Signals

1-click rating on some replies (👍 / 👎 / 🔥).

4.2 Control Charts

For each mode/channel:

Maintain a control chart on:

Acceptance rate

Misclassification rate

Define control limits:

Example:

If acceptance rate > 80% and misclassification < 5% for N messages, process is “in control.”

When in control:

Allow automation to increase:

Auto-send low-risk “Thank you!” style responses.

Auto-comment limited Agree & Amplify with random audits.

If process goes out of control:

Increase human oversight:

Require approval for everything again.

Trigger re-training / recalibration.

7. Non-Functional Requirements

Security

OAuth where possible.

Encrypted storage for tokens.

Role-based access if multi-user later (e.g., assistant can see queue but not settings).

Compliance & ToS

Respect platform automation rules.

Throttling and randomization to avoid “bot-like” behavior.

Performance

New messages processed & scored within ~30 seconds of arrival (target).

Explainability

For each priority score or response, user can see “why”:

“High priority because: job opportunity + matches past high-value patterns + from domain X.”
