# HIPAA Privacy and Security Policy
## MediCare Solutions, Inc.
### Policy Number: HIPAA-2024-001
### Effective Date: January 1, 2024
### Version: 3.0

---

## 1. POLICY STATEMENT

**"Keep it private, keep it safe"** - This is our organizational commitment. MediCare Solutions, Inc. is dedicated to protecting Protected Health Information (PHI) from the moment a patient walks through our door. All workforce members must ensure that patient information is never on display and must maintain PHI protection every single day.

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all workforce members from the janitor to the CEO. As stated in our training materials: "HIPAA compliance, everyone must know." This includes:
- All employees
- Business associates  
- Contractors and volunteers
- Anyone with access to PHI

## 3. PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION (PHI) DEFINITION

### 3.1 The 18 HIPAA Identifiers
PHI includes but is not limited to:
- Names and birthdays
- Addresses (geographic subdivisions smaller than state)
- Medical records and account numbers
- Social Security Numbers (SSN)
- Facial photos and biometric identifiers
- IP addresses
- All dates directly related to an individual
- Telephone and fax numbers
- Email addresses
- Medical record numbers
- Health plan beneficiary numbers
- Certificate/license numbers
- Vehicle identifiers
- Device identifiers and serial numbers
- Web URLs
- Full face photographic images
- Any unique identifying characteristics

*Remember: "P-H-I, what does it include? Names, locations, dates, don't be crude"*

## 4. PRIVACY SAFEGUARDS

### 4.1 Physical Safeguards
- **Lock your screen when you step away** - All workstations must be secured when unattended
- **Secure it tight** - Physical documents must be stored in locked cabinets
- **Shred those papers before they're gone** - All PHI documents must be shredded before disposal

### 4.2 Verbal Communications
The following locations are PROHIBITED for discussing PHI:
- **Hallways** - "Even in the hallway, watch what you say"
- **Elevators** - "Elevator conversation? That's a no"
- **Cafeterias/Lunch areas** - "At lunch discussing cases? Let it go"
- Any public area where unauthorized individuals may overhear

### 4.3 Electronic Communications
- **Social media patient photo share? Million dollar fine if you dare**
- **Texting patient data on your phone? Use approved channels alone**
- All electronic PHI must use encryption
- Passwords must be strong and unique
- Email containing PHI must be encrypted

## 5. MINIMUM NECESSARY STANDARD

Access to PHI shall be limited to:
- **"Need to know basis, that's the rule"**
- **"Minimum necessary, that's the tool"**
- Only information required to perform job duties
- Role-based access controls shall be implemented

## 6. AUTHORIZATION AND DISCLOSURE

### 6.1 Required Authorization
- **"Authorization forms before you share"**
- **"Double-check permissions everywhere"**
- Written authorization required for all non-treatment disclosures
- Verify authorization validity before any release

### 6.2 Documentation Requirements
- **"Document access in all the ways"**
- All access to PHI must be logged
- Audit trails must be maintained for 6 years

## 7. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 7.1 Timeline Requirements
- **"Breach notification, 60 days"** - All affected individuals must be notified within 60 days of breach discovery
- Department of Health and Human Services notification required
- Media notification for breaches affecting 500+ individuals

### 7.2 Incident Reporting
- **"Report suspicious activity today"**
- All suspected breaches must be reported immediately
- Contact IT security team for support
- **"When in doubt, just don't share"**

## 8. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

### 8.1 Frequency
- **"Training yearly, it's required by law"**
- All workforce members must complete annual HIPAA training
- New employees must be trained within 30 days of hire
- Additional training required when policies change

### 8.2 Training Content
Must cover:
- Privacy matters in every way
- Patient privacy is their right
- Keep it private, out of touch
- One violation is too much

## 9. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

### 9.1 Violation Consequences
**"Five million dollars or a year in jail, that's what happens when you fail"**

Violations may result in:
- Civil penalties ranging from $100 to $50,000 per violation
- Annual maximum penalties up to $2,000,000 per violation category
- Criminal penalties including imprisonment
- Immediate termination of employment
- Loss of professional licenses

### 9.2 Compliance Monitoring
- Regular audits will be conducted
- **"Better keep HIPAA on your mind"**
- **"One single breach and you're wrecked"**

## 10. SECURITY MEASURES

### 10.1 Access Controls
- **"Lock it, log off"** - Automatic logoff after 10 minutes of inactivity
- Unique user identification for each employee
- Automatic lock screens required
- **"Encryption on, passwords strong"**

### 10.2 Physical Security
- Facility access controls
- Workstation security measures
- Device and media controls
- Paper records must remain secure

## 11. PATIENT RIGHTS

Every patient has the right to:
- Privacy of their health information
- Access their medical records
- Request amendments to their records
- Request restrictions on uses and disclosures
- Choose how they receive health information
- **"Patient privacy is their right"**

## 12. WORKFORCE RESPONSIBILITIES

### 12.1 Individual Accountability
All workforce members must:
- **"Keep it private, keep it safe"**
- **"Every patient, every case - Respect it!"**
- Protect PHI at all times
- Report violations immediately
- **"Be smarter, be aware"**

### 12.2 Best Practices
- Think before you speak
- Verify before you share
- Secure before you leave
- **"IT security team is there"** - when you need help
- **"Keep those massive fines at bay"**
