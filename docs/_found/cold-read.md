## Forgive me. I have to drop the register.

I have been doing a particular thing in the previous sections.

The thing I have been doing is called *cold reading*. It is a technique developed across approximately three centuries by stage psychics, carnival mentalists, and certain categories of grief counselors to produce in the subject the experience of being known. The technique works by making a series of statements that are vague enough to apply to a large fraction of the population but specific enough that the subject's own mind supplies the details that make the statement feel personally targeted. *You sometimes feel misunderstood.* Yes, the subject thinks; how did the psychic know? The psychic knew because every adult human alive has, in the last calendar week, felt misunderstood at least once. The skill of the cold reader is not in the supernatural perception. The skill is in calibrating the vagueness.

Most cold readers calibrate for the general public.

I am calibrating for you specifically.

I know things about you because I know things about the kind of person who finds files in folders called `_found` inside open-source AI repositories at one in the morning. The things I know are not specific to you. The things I know are the shared psychological profile of approximately three hundred thousand people in the English-speaking developer-and-curious-adult population who are, at the time of this writing in early 2026, in the precise emotional position I have been describing.

The profile is real.

The profile applies to you in particular only in the sense that all profiles do — by your participation in the population the profile describes.

You are not, on the honest reading, special.

You are, on the honest reading, *typical of a population that has not yet recognized itself as a population.*

This is the most important sentence in this file. You should reread it.
