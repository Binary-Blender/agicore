## Description (Long — for KDP)

**The next strategic inflection has already arrived.**

It is the largest disruption in enterprise software since the industry's emergence — larger than the PC, larger than the cloud, larger than mobile. It is invisible to incumbents because its mechanics violate every Porter-era assumption MBA strategists were trained on. By the time the fins break the surface, the response window will have closed.

This is a graduate-level business textbook in the lineage of Christensen's *Innovator's Dilemma*, Grove's *Only the Paranoid Survive*, and McGrath's *Seeing Around Corners*. The book names what is coming, develops the substrate analysis that explains its mechanics, and prescribes the organizational response that distinguishes surviving firms from dissolved ones.

The book's load-bearing forecast: **within six months of publication, ten thousand individual operators will be running at approximately one hundred times the productive output baseline of their pre-AI selves, with the appearance to the rest of the industry that they materialized at once.** The forecast is grounded in observed propagation rates of AI-era capabilities — skill documents, agentic coding, deterministic-substrate architectures — that have repeatedly shown the phase-transition shape this book makes its centerpiece.

**The book's structure:**

**Part I — Seeing the Inflection.** Why the standard MBA toolkit produces blindness in the face of cognition-systems disruption. The boil metaphor as the controlling image of strategic foresight. McGrath, Christensen, and Grove sharpened for the new substrate.

**Part II — The Disruption Mechanics.** Cognition Systems Engineering as a named discipline. The determinism contract that closes the last enterprise objection to AI-built software. The cost collapse of approximately 100x — including a documented case at 693x. The one-person firm as the disruption vehicle. The seven structural traps that defeat incumbent responses. The corpus compounding — the super-exponential mechanism that makes the disruption's economics worsen for the incumbent at an accelerating rate, with the operator's corpus framed not as moat but as mountain.

**Part III — The Strategic Inflection.** Grove's *only the paranoid survive* register applied with severity. The labor-market untying that distributes senior practitioners across four archetypes. The Hartwell case — a Boston consultancy that did not respond — recast as MBA-classroom case material. The Stillwater contrast — a Minneapolis consultancy whose CEO read the boil and responded.

**Part IV — The Transformation.** Transformative AI Operations as the named transformation framework. The Multiplication Doctrine. The Blueprint Audit. The Stillwater perks-first first move. The Stillwater partners-not-resources second move — the chapter the book is built around. The ecosystem posture toward the disruptor population.

**Part V — Cases and Practice.** A worked ERP disruption from end to end, presented from both the disruptor's and the incumbent's perspective. The four-archetype typology of the post-inflection labor market — Priya, Ben, Damon, Aliyah. The new equilibrium three years after the inflection.

**For:** MBA and EMBA students, corporate strategists, boards of directors, C-suite of enterprise software incumbents, mid-market consultancy CEOs, and anyone whose firm's value depends on retaining senior talent the labor market has begun to untie.

**Required reading for the strategy and disruption seminars of the 2026-2027 academic year and onward.**
