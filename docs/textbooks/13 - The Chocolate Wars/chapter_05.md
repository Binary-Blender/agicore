# Chapter 5: Bentonville

Carla Hernandez called Jimmy on the secure line at eight oh-six AM Pacific on a Wednesday in late April of Year Nine, four weeks after the cease-and-desist video, two weeks after Lerato had flown back from KwaZulu-Natal, and one week after Karim Brock had told Jimmy, in a brief check-in over coffee on a Monday morning, that the Q1 Feastables sell-through numbers at Walmart had come in approximately seventeen percent above the operational forecast.

Jimmy was at his desk in the office at the Greenville studio.

He was reading a printout of the Q1 sell-through report that Karim had walked in twenty minutes earlier.

The phone rang.

The phone was the encrypted-line phone Karim had set up for Jimmy two years earlier when the volume of Feastables-related sensitive operational calls had crossed a threshold that the standard cell-phone setup had stopped being adequate for.

The phone display read: *Carla Hernandez — Walmart.*

Jimmy picked up.

He said: "Carla."

Carla said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Where are you."

She said: "My office in Bentonville. Eight oh-six AM Pacific your time. Ten oh-six AM Central my time. I have, in the previous nine minutes, walked across the corridor to the small office at the end of the floor that does not have any windows and that is, by the corporate-protocol Walmart has been operating under for the previous forty years, the office Walmart category managers use when they are making calls they do not want their colleagues to overhear. I am making this call from that office. I am telling you that I am making this call from that office because the call is the kind of call that, in operational terms, requires the office. The office is the office. I am going to be in it for the next ten minutes. The call is going to be ten minutes."

Jimmy said: "Carla."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "Tell me."

Carla told him.

She told him in fourteen sentences.

The fourteen sentences were:

1. The Hershey Company had, across the previous nine weeks, been running a multi-pronged retailer-relationship campaign explicitly designed to position Walmart to reduce Feastables' shelf allocation in the Q3 planogram review.

2. Marcus Whittaker, the CEO of the Hershey Company, had personally called Walmart's CEO three times across the previous five weeks.

3. Yvette Park, the Hershey Company's chief category officer, had called Carla's boss twice in the previous three weeks.

4. The Hershey Company had, in the previous six weeks, offered Walmart a series of co-marketing deals at terms Walmart had not been offered before — including a fifteen-percent off-invoice allowance on Hershey's full chocolate-bar portfolio for Q4, a guaranteed twenty-eight-percent shelf-allocation increase for Hershey's product across the Walmart chocolate aisle, and a one-time fourteen-million-dollar marketing-investment payment to fund a Walmart-exclusive Hershey's holiday-season activation.

5. The Hershey Company had, in the same six weeks, attached to each of those offers an informal request that Walmart consider reducing Feastables' shelf allocation in the Q3 planogram review by an amount the Hershey Company had not specifically quantified but that Carla, in her honest read of the conversations, estimated at between thirty and forty-five percent of Feastables' current chocolate-bar facings at Walmart.

6. Walmart had not, in any of the six weeks of conversations, accepted any of the Hershey Company's deals on the terms attached to them.

7. Walmart had not, in any of the same six weeks, told the Hershey Company that the deals were unacceptable on the terms attached to them.

8. Walmart had, instead, in the operational protocol Walmart used for situations of this kind, deferred each conversation to the next quarterly review window, which was the Q3 planogram review, which was the review the Hershey Company had been attempting to influence.

9. Walmart's chief merchant — Carla's boss's boss — had, on Tuesday afternoon of the previous week, in a closed-door meeting Carla had been in the room for, made a single statement about the Hershey-pressure campaign that Carla was now, in her own honest assessment of the operational situation, going to share with Jimmy on this call against Walmart's internal-communication protocol.

10. The statement the chief merchant had made was: *"Hershey's has, in the previous nine weeks, attempted to manipulate Walmart at a level Walmart has not been manipulated at by a single vendor in the previous twelve years. Walmart is not going to tolerate the manipulation. Walmart is also not going to publicly call out the manipulation, because publicly calling out the manipulation is not, in operational terms, Walmart's protocol. Walmart's protocol is to allocate shelf space on the basis of sell-through. Feastables' sell-through is, in Q1 of Year Nine, approximately thirty-one percent above the chocolate-bar category benchmark. Feastables is going to get its shelf allocation in the Q3 planogram review on the basis of the sell-through. Hershey's is going to be told what Hershey's is going to be told. The conversation is closed."*

11. Carla was calling Jimmy now because Carla was, on her own quiet read of the situation, the senior Walmart executive who had been the most exposed to the Hershey-pressure campaign across the previous nine weeks, who had taken the most personal heat for declining the Hershey-offered deals on the attached terms, and who was, on her own honest assessment of her professional position at Walmart, going to be the person whose career was most directly affected by whatever happened next in the Hershey-Feastables-Walmart triangulation.

12. Carla was telling Jimmy because Carla wanted Jimmy to know.

13. Carla was not telling Jimmy because Carla wanted Jimmy to do anything specific with the information. Carla was, in operational terms, telling Jimmy as a courtesy from one friend to another friend in an industry in which courtesies of this kind were, by Walmart's strict internal-communication protocol, not supposed to happen.

14. Carla was, by the close of the call, going to ask Jimmy not to share with anyone at Walmart that Carla had made the call, and Carla was, by the close of the call, going to ask Jimmy whether he wanted to come to Bentonville the following day to meet with Carla, with Carla's boss, and with Walmart's chief merchant in a sequence of meetings Carla had been pre-positioning across the previous seventy-two hours and that would, if Jimmy said yes to the meeting, take place on the Thursday afternoon of the same week.

She stopped.

Jimmy was quiet for two seconds.

He said: "Carla."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "Thank you."

She said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Yes."

She said: "Are you going to come."

He said: "I am going to come."

She said: "Tomorrow afternoon."

He said: "Tomorrow afternoon."

She said: "Two PM Central."

He said: "I will be there at one fifty."

She said: "Bring no one. The meeting is, by my own pre-positioning, a meeting that only works if you are in the room alone. If you bring anyone, the meeting will be the meeting, but it will be a different meeting. The meeting I have set up is a meeting in which the chief merchant is going to say something to you, in operational terms, that he is not going to say in the presence of anyone other than you. I am asking you to trust me on the staging."

He said: "I trust you on the staging."

She said: "Good."

She said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Yes."

She said: "I am going to hang up now."

He said: "Hang up."

She hung up.

Jimmy set the phone down.

He sat at his desk for approximately one minute.

He picked up the phone.

He called Karim.

He said: "Karim. Charter to Bentonville tomorrow morning. I want to be at Walmart's Bentonville office at one fifty PM Central tomorrow. I am going alone. No team. No security. Just me and the charter and the rental car from the airport."

Karim said: "Alone."

Jimmy said: "Alone."

Karim said: "Jimmy."

Jimmy said: "Yes."

Karim said: "The last time you flew alone to a major retailer was the Costco call in 2023. The call did not, by my own count, go badly. The call also did not, by my own count, go the way you had expected it to. I am, on my standard chief-of-production protocol, going to push back on the alone."

Jimmy said: "Karim."

Karim said: "Yes."

Jimmy said: "Carla pre-positioned the meeting. The meeting only works alone. I am going alone."

Karim said: "All right."

Jimmy said: "Karim."

Karim said: "Yes."

Jimmy said: "Thank you for pushing back."

Karim said: "You are welcome."

Karim hung up.

Jimmy called Lerato.

Lerato was at the small office of her production company in downtown Greenville.

She picked up.

She said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Lerato. I am flying to Bentonville tomorrow afternoon. I am going alone. I will be back tomorrow night."

She said: "Bentonville. Walmart."

He said: "Walmart."

She said: "All right."

He said: "I will be home tomorrow night."

She said: "All right."

He said: "Lerato."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "I called you because I am going to start telling you about the trips before I am on the plane instead of after I have landed. The telling is the telling. The model is the model. The model includes the telling. I am starting the telling now."

She was quiet for a beat.

She said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Yes."

She said: "Thank you."

He said: "You are welcome."

She said: "Bring back the meeting."

He said: "I will bring back the meeting."

She hung up.

---

The charter lifted from Pitt-Greenville at six oh-eight AM Eastern Thursday morning.

Jimmy was on board.

Jimmy was alone except for the two pilots and the small flight attendant who had been on Jimmy's charter staff for two years.

The flight to Northwest Arkansas Regional was three hours and eleven minutes.

The flight landed at eight nineteen AM Central.

The rental car was a black Suburban that, by Karim's quiet over-protocol that Jimmy had not specifically authorized but that Karim had set up regardless because Karim was, in operational posture, the kind of head of production who did not, on his honest assessment of Jimmy's solo-trip risk profile, send Jimmy to a major retailer's headquarters in a fully unmanaged rental car, had been pre-positioned at the airport by a small private security firm Karim had been quietly using since 2022.

Jimmy drove the Suburban himself.

He drove the twenty-eight minutes from the airport to the Walmart Home Office complex in Bentonville.

He parked in the visitor lot.

He walked into the lobby of the building Carla had pre-positioned for the meeting.

He checked in at the visitor desk.

The receptionist did not, in any way Jimmy could observe, react to his presence in the lobby beyond the standard visitor-check-in protocol.

The receptionist gave Jimmy a visitor badge.

The receptionist gave Jimmy a small printed map of the building floor on which the meeting was scheduled.

Jimmy walked to the meeting.

The meeting was on the third floor of the building.

The meeting was in a small conference room at the end of the south corridor.

The small conference room had three chairs at a round table.

Carla was at one of the chairs.

Carla's boss was at the second chair.

Walmart's chief merchant was not yet in the room.

Carla stood up.

She walked to Jimmy.

She embraced him briefly.

She said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Carla."

She said: "Thank you for coming."

He said: "Thank you for the call."

She said: "Jimmy. This is my boss, Diane Tashiro, senior vice president of consumables category management. Diane, this is Jimmy."

Jimmy shook Diane's hand.

Diane said: "Jimmy."

Jimmy said: "Diane."

Diane said: "Carla has told me what she told you. I am, on my own honest read of the operational situation, in agreement with Carla's read. I am here this afternoon because the chief merchant asked me to be here. The chief merchant is going to be in the room in approximately four minutes. The chief merchant is going to say something to you when he arrives. Carla and I are not going to say anything across the conversation. The conversation is going to be the chief merchant's conversation. After the conversation, the chief merchant is going to leave. Carla and I are going to walk you to the parking lot. That will be the meeting."

Jimmy said: "Diane."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "Thank you for the staging."

She said: "You are welcome."

They sat.

The chief merchant walked in at one fifty-eight PM Central.

The chief merchant was a fifty-eight-year-old man named Henry Calloway who had been at Walmart for thirty-one years, had been chief merchant for the previous six, and had been, by the small private read Jimmy had been able to assemble through Karim's network across the previous two years, the single most influential individual in the U.S. consumer-packaged-goods retail ecosystem.

Henry Calloway walked across the small conference room.

He shook Jimmy's hand.

He said: "Mr. Donaldson."

Jimmy said: "Mr. Calloway."

Henry said: "Please call me Henry."

Jimmy said: "Please call me Jimmy."

Henry said: "Jimmy."

Jimmy said: "Henry."

Henry sat down in the third chair.

He did not, across the next ninety seconds, say anything.

Jimmy did not, across the same ninety seconds, say anything.

Carla and Diane did not, across the same ninety seconds, say anything.

At the close of the ninety seconds, Henry said: "Jimmy. I am going to say one thing to you. The one thing is going to be the meeting. After I have said the one thing, the meeting is over. I want you to listen to the one thing. I want you to think about the one thing across the next ninety days. I want you to come back to me, in person, at this office, ninety days from today, with whatever response you have decided to bring. The one thing is the one thing. Are we clear."

Jimmy said: "We are clear."

Henry said: "The one thing is this."

He paused.

He said: "Walmart will not, under any circumstance, give Feastables less shelf space than your sell-through justifies. The Hershey pressure campaign of the previous nine weeks has been an attempt to manipulate Walmart that Walmart is not going to tolerate. Walmart is also not, on the standard Walmart protocol, going to publicly call out the manipulation. I am, however, going to tell you, in this room, that the manipulation has happened, that the manipulation will be remembered, and that Walmart is, in the Q3 planogram review, going to allocate Feastables an additional category-position increase of one full SKU across the chocolate-bar facing in approximately seven thousand four hundred and twenty stores nationwide. The SKU allocation is the SKU allocation. The allocation is being granted not because of any conversation we are having this afternoon, but because Feastables' sell-through justifies it. The conversation we are having this afternoon is, in operational terms, a separate conversation about whether the relationship between Feastables and Walmart is, across the next ten years, going to be the relationship I want it to be."

He paused again.

"The relationship I want it to be is the relationship in which Feastables continues to operate the way Feastables has been operating. The relationship I do not want it to be is the relationship in which Feastables becomes, in operational posture, the kind of CPG company that, having gained significant market share, begins to operate the way the legacy CPG companies operate. I am not going to manage the relationship from this side any differently than I would manage any other CPG relationship. I am asking you to manage the relationship from your side the way you have been managing it. Keep being the company that has been growing the way you have been growing. Do not become, in the next five years, the company that the Hershey Company has, across the previous fifteen years, become. The conversation is over."

He stood up.

He shook Jimmy's hand.

He walked out of the room.

The conversation had lasted four minutes and eleven seconds.

Carla and Diane stood up.

They walked Jimmy to the parking lot.

At the Suburban, Carla said: "Jimmy."

Jimmy said: "Carla."

Carla said: "You got the SKU."

Jimmy said: "I got the SKU."

Carla said: "Do not, in the next ten years, become the Hershey Company."

Jimmy said: "I am not going to become the Hershey Company."

Carla said: "Drive safely."

Jimmy drove safely.

He flew back to Greenville on the charter.

He was home at the house in Greenville at six forty-six PM Eastern Thursday night.

Lerato was at the kitchen table.

She had cooked.

He sat down across from her.

He told her about the meeting.

He told her about the SKU.

He told her about Henry Calloway's closing line.

Lerato listened.

When he finished, she said: "Jimmy."

He said: "Yes."

She said: "What did Henry Calloway tell you in the part you have not told me."

Jimmy looked at her.

He said: "Lerato."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "He told me not to become the Hershey Company."

She said: "I know he did."

He said: "How did you know."

She said: "Because the meeting was a four-minute meeting in a small conference room at the end of a corridor with the chief merchant of the largest retailer in the United States, who flew nobody else into the room. The meeting was not the SKU. The meeting was the warning. The warning is the warning."

He said: "Lerato."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "How do you keep doing this."

She said: "Jimmy. I have been a documentary filmmaker for nine years. I have, in the previous nine years, watched approximately three hundred and forty senior executives walk in and out of meetings on every continent. I have a calibrated read of what they say in rooms and what they say in the parking lot. The parking lot is the parking lot. Carla's *do not become the Hershey Company* was the parking lot. Henry Calloway's *the relationship I want it to be is the relationship in which Feastables continues to operate the way Feastables has been operating* was the room. The room is the warning. The parking lot is the friendship. You got both today. Most CEOs in your position get one in their career. You got both in a single afternoon. The warning is the warning, Jimmy. Do not become the Hershey Company."

Jimmy looked at her.

He said: "Lerato."

She said: "Yes."

He said: "I am not going to become the Hershey Company."

She said: "Good."

She picked up her fork.

She kept eating.

The chicken was good.

The carrots were good.

The potatoes were good.

The meeting was the meeting.

The warning was the warning.

The empire continued.

The ninety-day clock, by Lerato's quiet count, was at day forty-one and counting.

The architecture was still not the architecture.

Jimmy did not yet know it.

He had thirteen days remaining before he would.
