# Chapter 13: The Third Classroom

The announcement of the lead had been made, on a follow-up press release issued at six AM Eastern on the Monday morning of the second week of October — exactly four weeks after the first press release — that named, in its lead paragraph, *Eleanor Whitcomb, of the Class of 1972 of the Milton Hershey School, who served on the Milton Hershey School Trust's board for nineteen years through her resignation in May, who has been retained on a five-year operational mandate as the founding chief executive of the joint operational entity, and who will, on the operational schedule of the entity's first ninety days, be making site visits to the Beast Philanthropy school network in southern Africa to inform the joint entity's initial school-build program.*

Eleanor said: "The schedule is the schedule."

The vehicle was a Toyota Hilux double-cab pickup that the Beast Philanthropy regional office had used as its primary site vehicle since Year Seven, with one hundred and eighty-three thousand kilometers on the odometer, two scratches along the left front fender that Lerato had not, on her own honest accounting, had repaired because the scratches were the scratches the vehicle had earned in its operational work and did not, on Lerato's operational accounting, diminish the vehicle, and a small white sticker on the rear window that read, in the standard Beast Philanthropy program-identification format: *Beast Philanthropy — Eighth School — KwaZulu-Natal.*

The conversation in the vehicle was, in its first hour, about the road.

The conversation in the vehicle was, in its second hour, about Lerato's documentary work prior to Beast Philanthropy.

The conversation in the vehicle was, in its third hour, about Eleanor's nineteen years on the Trust board.

The conversation in the vehicle was, in its fourth hour, about Eleanor's own three years at the Milton Hershey School, between 1968 and 1972, during which she had been one of the first eleven young women admitted to the school.

The third classroom was the classroom on the south side of the building, with the two south-facing windows and the one east-facing window and the small built-in storage closet on the back wall that Thandi had added on her own design initiative.

The third classroom held, at one fourteen PM local time on the Tuesday, twenty-seven students between the ages of nine and eleven.
