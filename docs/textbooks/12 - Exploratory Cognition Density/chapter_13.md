# Chapter 13: Skills That Stack Well in the AI Era

This chapter is a list.

The list is sixteen items long. Each item is a skill, paired with the AI capability the skill pairs against. The pairing is the leverage. The skill, on its own, is fine. The skill plus the AI capability is the rare combinatorial position.

I am not going to make you wait for the list. The list is the chapter. Here it is.

---

**1. Clear written communication + AI prompting and drafting.**

The AI writes. You edit. You shape. You decide what is being said. You add the judgment. The AI cannot tell you what to say. The AI can only tell you how to say it well, once you have decided what to say. The decision is the skill. The decision is, in 2026, one of the most consistently undersupplied skills in the labor market.

**2. Cross-domain reading + AI semantic search.**

You have read widely. You can recognize when a problem in one field is the same problem, structurally, as a problem in another field. The AI can find the relevant papers in eleven seconds across forty libraries. The AI cannot tell you that the problem in your field is the same problem as the problem in a different field, because the AI does not know what your field is until you tell it. The cross-domain recognition is the skill. The retrieval is the AI's job.

**3. Visual taste + AI image generation.**

You can look at three generated images and pick the one that is going to work. The AI can produce eight thousand images in an hour. The taste is the skill. The taste is, in 2026, the binding constraint on every visual workflow at every company that has not figured out that the binding constraint is the taste.

**4. Workflow design + AI automation.**

You can look at a process — a sales pipeline, a customer-onboarding sequence, a quarterly close, a hiring pipeline — and identify where the work actually lives, where it gets stuck, what is essential and what is theatrical. The AI can build the automation in a weekend. The workflow design is the skill. Most automation projects fail because the workflow was never analyzed. The AI does not analyze workflows. The AI implements them. You analyze.

**5. Customer-language fluency + AI customer interaction.**

You have, over the course of your career, talked to enough customers that you know how they actually describe their problems, which is almost never the way the engineering team describes the same problems. The AI can handle the volume of customer interactions. The AI cannot, at present, translate between customer-language and engineering-language at scale without somebody who has lived in both. You have lived in both. You translate.

**6. Reading dashboards critically + AI dashboard generation.**

You can look at a dashboard and identify, in ninety seconds, which metrics are real and which are theatrical. The AI can generate dashboards on demand. Most dashboards in most companies are theatrical. The skill of reading them is rare. The skill of building them is now cheap. The combination is, in 2026, one of the highest-leverage positions in any operations organization.

**7. Pattern recognition in organizational behavior + AI process modeling.**

You can read a company the way Marv reads a parking lot. The AI can build a process model of how the company functions. The combination is the new internal-consulting role. The internal consultant who reads the lot and builds the model is going to do the work that twelve external McKinsey consultants used to bill three million dollars for. The internal consultant will, by 2030, be a permanent role at every Fortune 1000 company that survives.

**8. Architectural judgment in complex systems + AI code generation.**

You can decide what the system should look like. The AI writes the system. The decision is the skill. The decision used to be made implicitly in the act of writing the code, line by line, by the senior engineer who held the architecture in her head. The AI now writes the code without needing to hold the architecture. The architecture, therefore, has to be held by a human who can read what the AI generated and recognize whether the AI built the right thing. This is one of the four or five most valuable skills in software engineering in the AI era. Most engineering job descriptions still do not list it.

**9. Negotiation + AI deal structuring.**

You can sit across the table from another person and arrive at a deal that both of you can live with. The AI can structure the deal, draft the contract, simulate the financial outcomes, and identify the precedents. The AI cannot do the negotiation. The negotiation is the meeting. The meeting is the skill. The meeting will, in 2026, still require you.

**10. Original taste in any creative field + AI generation in the same field.**

This is item 3, generalized. The taste is the skill. The taste does not have to be world-class. The taste has to be *distinctive enough* that the AI cannot replicate it from a generic prompt. Distinctive taste, applied to AI generation, is the new creative-class career. There are going to be ten thousand of these careers by 2030 in fields that did not have careers before.

**11. Direct sales + AI lead generation.**

The AI can identify ten thousand potential customers. The AI can draft personalized outreach to all ten thousand. The AI cannot close. The close is the skill. Direct salespeople with the temperament for cold conversation, in 2026, are among the most consistently undersupplied workers in the United States. The ones who pair their temperament with AI lead generation are running businesses.

**12. Teaching + AI personalization.**

You can teach a concept to a real person and adjust on the fly when the person looks confused. The AI can personalize curriculum at scale based on telemetry. The pairing is the new teacher. The new teacher works with twelve students at a time, each on a different track, each receiving AI-personalized practice while the teacher does the human work of recognizing where each student is actually stuck. This is the future of formal education. The Stupid Company™ version of education will be AI-only. The Wise Corporation version will be teacher-plus-AI. The Wise Corporation version will produce better outcomes by a factor of three within a decade. (I am told the same factor applies in corporate training. I have not personally verified.)

**13. Project management + AI status tracking.**

The AI tracks who is doing what, when, with whom, and at what blocker. You make the decisions about reprioritization, scope, and trade-offs that the AI cannot make because the decisions involve judgment about politics, fatigue, motivation, and timing. The project management role survives the AI era. The project management role is, in fact, *better* in the AI era, because the AI eats the tedious parts and leaves the judgment parts.

**14. Hands-on physical-world skill of any kind + AI work-augmentation tools.**

Plumbing. Electrical work. Carpentry. Auto repair. Veterinary medicine. Dental hygiene. Surgery. Cooking. Tailoring. Sailing. None of these are going away. All of these are getting AI-augmented in the diagnostic, scheduling, training, and customer-facing dimensions. The hands-on practitioner who learns to use the AI tools is going to outearn their AI-illiterate peers by a wide margin starting in approximately 2027. The hands-on practitioner who does not learn the tools will still earn a living, but the gap will widen. The hands-on practitioner who is *also* good at one of the other items on this list — say, customer-language fluency or teaching — is going to run the business.

**15. Recognizing institutional dysfunction + AI productivity analysis.**

You can read a company and tell whether it is, in operational terms, the Stupid Company™ or the Wise Corporation. The AI can run productivity analyses on every team, every project, every individual. The combination is the new chief of staff. The new chief of staff is going to be the most valuable role on the executive team across the next decade. Most current chiefs of staff do not have this combination. The ones who do are going to be running their companies inside ten years.

**16. Managing your own anxiety in a career transition + every AI tool that exists.**

This is the most personally important item on the list. The skill is the meta-skill that lets you act on what the previous fifteen items have taught you. The skill is the ability to recognize that the labor market is changing under your feet, to identify which of the previous fifteen items you can credibly stack, and to act on the identification without paralyzing yourself with fear about the parts you cannot control. The skill is what separates the workers who navigate the AI era from the workers who get navigated by it. The skill is not taught in any school. The skill is, in my honest experience, learned by going through one significant career transition with somebody who has been through it before, and surviving. After you have survived one, you can do it again with much less anxiety.

---

That is the list.

Sixteen items.

I made it sixteen because I do not believe the Adams rule that eleven is funnier than ten applies to lists you intend to be useful. Sixteen has the right number of items to feel comprehensive without exhausting the reader. The list has, by my honest count across the twenty-eight engineers I have personally talked to who have read the draft, been called *comprehensive* nineteen times and *insufficient* twice. The two insufficient calls were both for skills the engineers had personal mastery of and felt I should have added. The skills they suggested are good skills. They are not on the list. The list is mine.

You can add your own.

You should add your own.

The list is a starting point.

---

I want to flag one important pattern.

Look at the sixteen items.

The pattern is that *every single skill on the list is a human-judgment skill paired with an AI capability that does not have judgment.*

That is the underlying structure of the entire list.

The underlying structure is: **AI does what AI does well, you do what AI does badly, and the leverage is the combination.**

The skill you are stacking is not a skill that competes with AI. The skill you are stacking is a skill that *completes* AI. The AI is the implementation. You are the judgment.

This is the entire game.

If you have been reading career advice on Twitter from venture capitalists, you have been told that you need to *master AI tools.* This is half-correct. Mastering the AI tool is not the skill. The AI tool is going to be mastered in nine minutes by any reasonably literate person inside the next three years. The skill is the *human judgment* the tool is paired against.

You already have the human judgment in some specific dimension. You may not have been counting it. You may have been told, for the previous twenty years of your career, that the judgment was not a skill — that it was just *common sense*, or *taste*, or *experience*.

It was a skill.

It still is.

It is, in the AI era, the only kind of skill that matters.

Then come back and re-read this list, because this list is the answer to the diagnostic, and the answer is going to be the thing you carry into your next conversation about your career.
