## Description (KDP — book detail page)

In 1984 Mark Sawusch published *101 Things to Do with Your Commodore 64*. The book taught a generation of kids that the right way to understand a computer is to type a small program into it, run it, and see what happens. The kids who typed in Sawusch's programs grew up to build the AI systems that defined the modern era.

*From Eliza to ChatGPT* is that book for AI.

Twelve chapters trace the history of artificial intelligence from Joseph Weizenbaum's Eliza (1966) through Claude Code (2024). Each chapter tells the story of one flagship system — Eliza, SHRDLU, MYCIN, Cyc, Deep Blue, Watson, AlexNet, AlphaGo, GPT-3, ChatGPT, and Claude Code — covering who built it, when, where, what it did, and what the field learned from it.

Each chapter then ends with a working reference implementation the reader compiles and runs on their own laptop, using the open-source Agicore framework and the bundled agicore-examples repository. The final two chapters walk the reader through running BabyAI as a private cooperative inference layer and using Claude Code to author a brand-new system from a plain-English description.

By the end of the book the reader has compiled and run twelve working AI systems spanning every major era of the field — and has more hands-on AI experience than most working AI engineers had on the day ChatGPT launched.

**For** the curious software engineer, the teacher with a smart class, the parent with a smart kid, the working professional who wants the field's actual history without the marketing varnish, and anyone who has read a thousand AI think-pieces and is finally ready to *build* one of the things.

**Prerequisites:** a laptop running macOS, Windows, or Linux. A willingness to open a terminal. A willingness to be wrong. The early chapters require no LLM API key; chapters ten through twelve require one (five dollars of credit at any frontier provider is sufficient).

**Companion to** *The Chocolate Wars* (Volume 13) — the business-thriller case study in which a YouTube creator rebuilds his thirteen-LLC empire on top of the same Agicore framework this book uses.

Fourteenth volume on the AI WIN-WIN Institute textbook shelf.
