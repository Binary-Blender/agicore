> *My father, John Bender, was a refrigeration technician at the Sigman Meat Company in West Plains, Missouri, from 1962 until he died of a cancer the OSHA forms told him he did not have, at age fifty-three, in 1998. I was twenty-three. I found his notebook in the bottom of his toolbox a week after the funeral. The notebook covered the years 1971 through 1997. The notebook had, on its careful pages, a record of every ammonia leak my father had observed at the plant. The leaks were each minor. The leaks were each unremarkable. The leaks added up, on a fifty-three-year-old man whose lungs had been on the breathing end of them for twenty-six years, to the cancer that killed him. The cancer was not in the OSHA forms. The cancer was in the notebook. The notebook was in the toolbox. Nobody at the plant read the notebook. Nobody at the plant asked. Nobody at the plant could have asked, because nobody at the plant had a tool for asking, and the notebook was not a tool the plant had access to. I have spent twenty-seven years building the tool that would have, in 1971, let my father's notebook reach somebody who could have done something about it. This pamphlet is the first piece of that tool that I can put directly into the hand of the person who was going to be my father if I had not built the tool. I am sorry it is twenty-seven years late. I am glad it is no longer that. — C.B.*
